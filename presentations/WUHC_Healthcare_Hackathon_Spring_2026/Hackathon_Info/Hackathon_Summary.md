## Overview

The WUHC Healthcare Hackathon is a two-week virtual competition that brings together passionate students to design innovative solutions to real-world healthcare challenges. Participants work individually or in teams to develop either a research-driven proposal or a technical solution, culminating in a final submission that includes a written proposal, a video pitch, and a visual summary.

The 2026 hackathon was organized by **Siddharth Adda, Ingrid Chan, Jaden Franks, Sarah Bat, Jai Hokimi, and Anya Jain**, and featured guest speaker **Dr. Ryan Leone** — an incoming Emergency Medicine resident at Brooke Army Medical Center and Columbia Medical School graduate, with a background in defense health policy at the Department of Defense, Department of State, and Department of Homeland Security.

> **Note:** No prior coding or programming experience is required. This hackathon is more of an "ideathon" — participants create and refine new ideas, but functioning apps or websites are not required (though welcome).

## Tracks

### 1. AI and Innovation
Build an AI program/model addressing ANY healthcare problem or disease. Can also research and present how a recent development in AI could help advance treatment and diagnosis for a particular disease.

**Context:** With a projected global shortage of 11 million health workers by 2030, AI models are increasingly integrating into the medical field. Participants should develop a prototype or study that addresses a specific healthcare challenge, prioritizing medical responsiveness and safe, practical implementation.

**How this could look:**
- Identify a specific dataset (e.g., MIMIC-III, Kaggle healthcare sets) and develop a diagnostic classifier, predictive model, or NLP tool for clinical note summarization.
- Select an AI architecture (e.g., Transformers, Multimodal AI) and conduct a Feasibility & Impact Study mapping how the technology integrates into clinical workflow.

---

### 2. Cancer
Research, synthesize, and discuss current/developing cancer treatments. Can be related to innovation, immunotherapy, radiotherapy, etc.

**Context:** There is a troubling rise in early-onset cancers (diagnosed under age 50), particularly colorectal, breast, and uterine variants. Nearly 90% of chemotherapy failures and over 50% of targeted therapy failures are attributed to acquired resistance. With 1 in 6 childhood cancer survivors facing major cardiovascular events by age 50, novel precision approaches are urgently needed.

**How this could look:**
- Propose a novel combination of existing therapies (e.g., immunotherapy paired with a metabolic inhibitor to counter drug resistance).
- Design a precision delivery proposal focused on a specific technology (like ADCs or localized radiotherapy) for a specific patient population such as pediatric or early-onset patients.

---

### 3. Healthcare Equity and Access — Democratic Republic of Congo
Design an innovative solution or proposal that tackles key social determinants of health or ongoing health crises in the **Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC)**.

**Key health crises in DRC:**
- **Monkeypox** — Over 100,000 cases and 2,000 deaths as of December 2025 (WHO)
- **Measles** — 80,000 cases in 2025 despite vaccination (Doctors Without Borders)
- Cholera and Ebola

**How this could look:**
- Identify a key health challenge linked to social determinants (limited rural access, food insecurity, maternal health disparities, etc.).
- Analyze underlying barriers using WHO, UNICEF, or academic study data.
- Design a practical, scalable intervention such as a community-based healthcare delivery model or a policy proposal addressing systemic barriers.

## Judging Rubric

| Criteria | Points | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Problem | /5 | How clearly and rigorously the team defines, understands, and validates a relevant healthcare problem using research, clinical insight, or real user needs |
| Innovation | /5 | The originality and creativity of the solution — how novel, disruptive, or thought-provoking it is compared to existing approaches |
| Technical Feasibility | /10 | How realistic and well-developed the solution is — through a viable prototype or a well-grounded, actionable proposal with clear implementation pathways |

**What makes a strong project:**
- Clear problem-solution fit
- Evidence-based research (user interviews, PubMed papers, articles, GitHub for code)
- A high-level product design or proposal overview
- Consideration of: Implementation, Feasibility, Scalability, Ethics, Clinical Value, Politics/Economics (especially Track 3)
- Bonus: Strong storytelling and visual design
